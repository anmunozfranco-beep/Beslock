# Manual Image Reset Policy

## Effective date
2026-05-13

## Purpose
Reset the Beslock manual-imagery program so new production starts from a clean baseline and no longer inherits the earlier realistic, lifestyle, or human-led visual direction.

## Non-negotiable rules
- New manual imagery must use only `schematic` or `hybrid` classes.
- Do not generate or approve full human figures, faces, or body-led scenes.
- Human presence is allowed only as a simplified hand silhouette or hand-only cue when the instructional step truly needs contact context.
- Within a single product package, the edge locking mechanism must remain consistent across all imagery that exposes the door edge, latch, deadbolt, rim box, or lock body.
- Exterior hardware, interior hardware, and edge hardware must be treated as separate views. Do not show interior and exterior lock bodies simultaneously in one manual image.

## Operational consequences
- Earlier `realistic` and `semi-realistic` assets are legacy material only; do not use them as the baseline for new generation decisions.
- Existing published `web-ready` files may remain in the repo until replacements exist, but they are quarantined from the new generation baseline.
- Every product matrix must declare the side or view for each slot: `exterior`, `interior`, `edge`, or `neutral`.
- Every product matrix must include at least one `edge-mechanism-reference` slot so the lock body, latch, or bolt family is fixed before broader production resumes.

## Editorial priorities
- Preserve product geometry before styling.
- Prefer simplified manual clarity over scene richness.
- Keep backgrounds quiet, diagram-like, or lightly contextual.
- Leave text labels, arrows, and numbered callouts for later editorial composition rather than baking them into the generated image.

## Reset intent
This reset is a workflow restart, not a live-site teardown.

Until replacement assets exist, published manual packages can remain available as legacy outputs.
New image generation, selection, scoring, and review work must begin from the reset rules in this document.
