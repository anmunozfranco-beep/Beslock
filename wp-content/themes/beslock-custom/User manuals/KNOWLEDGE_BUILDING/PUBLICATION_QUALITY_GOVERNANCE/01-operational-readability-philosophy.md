# Operational readability philosophy

A governed manual is not measured by what it contains, but by what an operator can do with it under operational pressure. Readability is a property of the rendered surface, not of the knowledge-core.

Principles:

- The knowledge-core is the source of truth; readability is its rendered projection.
- Readability is audience-conditioned; one profile renders at a time.
- Readability defects are surfaced, never silently fixed by the renderer.
- Readability never licenses content omission outside an explicit audience profile.
