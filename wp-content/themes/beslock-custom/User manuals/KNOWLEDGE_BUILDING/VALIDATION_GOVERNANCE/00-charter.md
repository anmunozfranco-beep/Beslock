# 00 — Validation Governance Charter

## Principles
1. Validation does not author knowledge; it observes and reports.
2. Operational integrity is a precondition for promotion to verified-truth.
3. Every validator is read-only; findings produce reports, not mutations.
4. Findings are typed (class), located (product+id+domain), and explainable.
5. Critical findings (unresolved-reference, lineage-break, promotion-without-evidence) have zero tolerance.
6. Validation is continuous; thresholds are governed, not negotiated.
7. Validation is subordinate to the knowledge-core and to LIFECYCLE_GOVERNANCE.
8. Future systems (RAG, chatbot, onboarding, troubleshooting) inherit validation results, never override them.

## Authority areas
- procedural-integrity
- workflow-executability
- entity-consistency
- graph-validation
- experience-validation
- retrieval-validation
- maturity-validation
- health-scoring
- validation-philosophy
- future-system-safety

## Hard guarantees
- Read-only. Modifies no per-product knowledge.
- Idempotent. Same inputs => same findings.
- Subordinate. Cannot override knowledge-core or lifecycle decisions.
- Non-destructive. Findings never delete artifacts.
