# e-Prime Visual Profile

## Reference anchor
- Current canonical reference image: `../e-Prime.png`
- The current anchor is a transparent isolated cutout; treat the alpha silhouette as the product boundary for new compositions.
- Product family: slim smart lever lock with full-height vertical plate

## Canonical identity
- very slim long-plate silhouette with a glossy black exterior front face
- straight lever handles centered in the vertical body
- upper front area includes a keypad or touch-control field integrated into the glossy plate
- a blue accent ring appears at the lever hub on the exterior side
- the front body also includes a lower oval insert below the handle
- interior side stays more matte and mechanical with a lower thumbturn area

## Protected geometry
- keep the ultra-slim plate proportions
- keep the glossy exterior face as the main visual identity cue
- keep the lever centered in the plate body
- keep the blue-ring interaction point at the lever hub, not at the handle tip
- preserve the lower oval insert on the front body

## Forbidden variations
- no knob conversion
- no camera cluster
- no face-recognition screen
- no fingerprint sensor moved to the handle tip
- no split exterior and interior modules

## Interaction zones
- upper front keypad or touch area
- lever-hub sensor zone with blue accent ring
- lower front oval insert only when a source step specifically needs it
- interior lower thumbturn area for inside-facing steps

## Supplemental OEM geometry
- short supplemental OEM sheet labels this family as `P14`
- the interior panel includes a battery compartment, battery cover, inside thumbturn, mounting screw points, reset button, and optional networking module area
- the exterior panel includes keypad, fingerprint zone, IC card area, mechanical key access, and emergency 5V power input
- the square spindle should protrude `10 to 30 mm` from the door surface after prep
- installation source confirms front and rear panels plus lock body are aligned by spindle, data cable, and lock bar during assembly

## Source caution
- `e-Prime_1.pdf` is the best current source for installation geometry and mounting sequence
- `e-Prime_2.pdf` mostly confirms the longer OEM manual already reflected in `generated_manuals/e-prime/manual.md`
- the short sheet claims total capacity of `300 users`, which conflicts with the older OCR summary of `100 users`; do not hard-code that conflict into customer-facing claims without validation

## Lighting and realism
Use soft reflections to keep the glossy plate edges visible without washing out the product.
Avoid merging e-Prime with e-Flex; the slimmer plate and lever-hub ring are key identity cues.
